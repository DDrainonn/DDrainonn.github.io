FAQ

Here is the place where new-comers to Compass can have their questions answered.
One of the most frequenly asked questions about compass relates to the scheduling. For that I will refer you to this link

How does the class length effect the learning enviroment?
If you don't already know all of the required classes at Compass are 2 hours long. This is different from the normal 1 hour classes that most students would be used to. Asking around the school you will find out that almost all of the student body thinks the 2 hour classes are no big deal. At Compass we have integrated classes that count for more credits than one normal class. This is why we have 2 hour classes, the teachers are teaching the equivalent of 2 classes.

What Kind of School is Compass?
Compass is a public school that gets governmental funding. There is a catch however. In order to be accepted into Compass you have to be entered into a raffle where only 150 students are accepted at a time. If you have a brother or sister that already goes to Compass however, then you get a higher chance of getting in.

Is a Computer for Every Student Wasting Money?
A large concern for many people coming into Compass and even people who don't go to school but still pay taxes is if Compass is wasting money on Computers for every stundent. The answer is no, no Compass is not wasting money on computers for each student. A normal textbook that would be bought by a normal highschool can cost up to $600 which is about the same price as the computers that we use. The computers have the added benifit of not going out of date in 2 years also.
